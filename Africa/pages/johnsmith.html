John Smith

The Forgotten Soldier from Sierra Leone

John Henry Smythe was born and raised in Freetown, the capital of Sierra Leone. Before joinen the RAF (Royal Air Force) in 1940, John was then working as a civil servant.

On November 18, 1942, on his twenty-eighth mission over Europe, his bomber was shot down and he was wounded in the groin and abdomen. Enemy troops found him hiding in a barn. Smythe later recalled: “The Germans couldn’t believe their eyes. I’m sure that’s what saved me from being shot immediately. To see a black man — an officer at that — was more than they could come to terms with. They just stood there gazing.” (blackpast, 2015)

In 1951, Smythe became a practicing barrister, married his Grenadian fiancée, Violet Wells Bain in London, and then moved back to Freetown.(blackpast, 2015) Smythe moved back to the UK with his family in 1993 after retiring from public service in Sierra Leone.

John Smythe died in Thame, Oxfordshire on July 9, 1996 and was buried in St. Mary’s Church Cemetery. He was survived by his wife and five children.(blackpast, 2015)
