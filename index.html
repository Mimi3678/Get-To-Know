Nelson Mandela

The freedom figther

1918 - On July 18th 1918, Nelson Mandela was born

1939 - Mandela started in the elite university of Fort Hare in South Africa.

1944 - andela joined the African National Congress (ANC) and worked with fellow party members. That same year, he met and married his first wife, Evelyn Ntoko Mase (1922-2004), with whom he had four children before their divorce in 1957.

1956 - On December 5, 1956, Mandela and 155 other activists were arrested and went on trial for treason

1961 - Nelson Mandela co-founded and became the first leader of Umkhonto we Sizwe (“Spear of the Nation”), also known as MK, a new armed wing of the ANC.

Years Behind Bars

Nelson Mandela spent the first 18 of his 27 years in jail at the brutal Robben Island Prison. He was only allowed to see his wife, Winnie Madikizela-Mandela (1936-), who he had married in 1958 and was the mother of his two young daughters, once every six months. In 1982 Mandela was moved to Pollsmoor Prison on the mainland, and in 1988 he was placed under house arrest on the grounds of a minimum-security correctional facility. The following year, newly elected president F. W. de Klerk (1936-) lifted the ban on the ANC and called for a nonracist South Africa, breaking with the conservatives in his party. On February 11, 1990, he ordered Mandela’s release.

Freedom after 27 years behind bars

In 1991, Nelson Mandela was elected the president of the African National Congress (ANC). On 10 May 1994, Nelson Mandela, at the age of 77, was inaugurated as South Africa’s first black president

Nelson Mandela died on December 5, 2013 from a recurring lung infection. In 1993, Mandela and President de Klerk were awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their work towards abolishing apartheid. Negotiations between black and white South Africans prevailed. On 27 April 1994, South Africa held its first democratic elections.
